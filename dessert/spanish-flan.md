# [<-](../README.md) Spanish Flan

## Ingredients
| Ingredients |             |
|-------------|-------------|
| 200ml       | Milk        |
| 1           | Egg (medium)|
| 2x 15g      | White sugar |
| 5g          | Water       |



## Method

### Step 1
Put half the sugar in a saucepan with a small amount of water to caramelize it.\
Very little, enough to wet the sugar but not enough to dissolve it.\
Put the lid on and don't lift it until the caramel is the color you want.\
Use a medium heat so that the caramel can be more caramelized before it has a burnt taste.\
After a while all the sugar will start to melt and the caramel will start to brown.\
A lighter color will taste sweeter and darker will taste more bitter.

### Step 2
Remove from the heat, add the water and let the caramel melt.\
Put the lid back on after adding the water.\
It should be liquid when it is hot. When it has cooled a little, it should have a honey-like consistency.
But pour it in while it is still hot so that it is easier to spread.

### Step 3
Pour the melted caramel into two aluminum containers.\
Cover the entire bottom.

### Step 4
Boil the milk in the same saucepan, which will probably have some caramel left.

### Step 5
Whisk the eggs with the other half of the sugar in another bowl, large enough to hold all the eggs and milk, while the milk heats up.\
Do not whisk to a foam. There should be no air bubbles.

### Step 6
When the milk has come to a boil, pour it all over the beaten eggs, stirring all the time!\
Again. Do not whisk to a foam. You do not want any air bubbles.

### Step 7
Divide the milk and egg mixture into the two containers.\
Pass the mixture through a sieve to remove any foam and egg clumps.

### Step 8
Cover both containers with aluminum foil, sealing them airtight.

### Step 9
Steam for 20 minutes at 90 to 95°C.
The temperature of the steamer will drop to about 80°C.
It will rise again in the first 10 minutes.
Do not exceed 95°C! Use a thermometer.

- !!! If you exceed 95°C, the eggs start to separate from the liquid and you end up with scrambled eggs in a watery caramel soup.

- If you are steaming on a stove, it is easier to preheat the steamer to 90-95 degrees Celsius and set the stove to maintain this temperature.\
  Then you add your flan. This way it will not reach more than 95 degrees Celsius.

- Any other device capable of reaching and holding 90°C is suitable for this procedure.\
  A good example: an air fryer with a dry function. It will not work as fast as steaming, as the dry air doesn't transfer heat as well as steam, but it works.\
  In an air fryer, the cooking time was about 1 1/2 hours.

### Step 10
Refrigerate for 4 hours.
This step is necessary to ensure all the caramel has melted.
