# [<-](../README.md) Italian Chocolate Mousse

## Ingredients
| Ingredients |                      |
|-------------|----------------------|
| 40g         | Egg white            |
| 10g         | Sugar                |
| 20g         | Dark chocolate       |
| 5g          | Butter (or other fat)|

## Method

### 1. Step
Melt the butter and dark chocolate together over a hot water bath (bain-marie).  
Do not let the mixture exceed 50°C. If it gets hotter, the chocolate can separate and the texture becomes grainy and fatty.

### 2. Step
In a second bowl over a hot water bath, heat the egg white and sugar to 50°C while stirring gently.  
By preheating the egg mixture, the liquid chocolate will not start to set or form lumps when you mix them together.

### 3. Step
Remove the egg white mixture from the heat and whip it until it is airy and no longer liquid.  
Because of the sugar, the exact stiffness is not critical. Anything from soft to firmer peaks works, as long as it is not runny.  
For easiest mixing with the chocolate, aim for soft peaks.

### 4. Step
Combine both mixtures. You can either:
- Gently fold with a spatula, or  
- Mix briefly with an electric hand mixer on low speed for about 2 seconds.  
Do not mix for too long or too vigorously, otherwise too much air escapes and the mousse becomes noticeably denser.

### 5. Step
Fill the mousse into serving dishes and refrigerate for about 3–4 hours.

## Remarks
- Keeping both mixtures around 50°C prevents the chocolate from seizing or starting to set when combined.
- You can replace butter with other fats (e.g. coconut oil), but this changes the "mouth warmth" and the flavor profile.
- The longer and harder you mix when combining chocolate and foam, the more air you lose and the denser the final mousse becomes.
