# [<-](../README.md) Cheese Cake Souffle

## Ingredients
| Ingredients |                   |
|-------------|-------------------|
| 150ml       | Cream cheese      |
| 3           | Eggs              |
| 50g         | Milk              |
| 65g         | Erythritol        |
| 10g         | Cornstarch        |
| 25g         | Cake flour        |
| 2g          | Salt              |
| 8g          | Lemon juice       |

## Method

### 1. Step
Separate the eggs. For best results, ensure all ingredients are at room temperature.

### 2. Step
In a mixing bowl, beat the egg yolks with half of the erythritol until the mixture becomes visibly lighter in color.

### 3. Step
Add the cream cheese, cake flour, and cornstarch to the yolk mixture. Mix until a smooth batter forms.

### 4. Step
Gradually add the milk while stirring continuously to avoid lumps. The resulting mixture should have a pudding-like consistency, not too runny.

### 5. Step
In a separate bowl, add the salt and lemon juice to the egg whites just before beating. Whip the egg whites, preferably with an electric mixer, until a light foam forms. Then gradually add the remaining half of the erythritol and continue whipping until soft peaks form. The meringue should be elastic and not overbeaten. If it breaks or separates, it has been whipped too long. The ideal consistency is when the whisk can be lifted out and a peak forms that gently bends over (soft peaks).

### 6. Step
Gently fold the meringue into the batter until no visible streaks remain.

### 7. Step
Pour the batter slowly into a round, suitable baking dish. If using a metal dish, line the bottom with parchment paper but silicone molds are prefered. Tap the dish gently on the counter to release any large air bubbles.

### 8. Step
Place the dish in a cold oven. Bake at 140°C for 40 minutes. Do not preheat the oven.

### 9. Step
After baking, turn off the oven and let the cake cool inside the oven to 40–50°C without opening the door. Opening the oven too soon will cause the cake to collapse and lose its airy texture.

## Remarks
If a large crack forms during baking, possible reasons include:
- The meringue was overbeaten.
- The oven temperature was too high.
- The oven radiates too much heat from above. Placing a baking tray on the top rack can help shield the cake from direct heat.

If the cake is too dense, possible reasons include:
  - The meringue was overmixed when folding into the batter.
  - The batter (before adding meringue) was too runny. If you cannot change the ingredients, try using less milk.
